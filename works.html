NOTABLE WORKS

Gapô (1988)

Overview:

Gapô is a novel set in Olongapo City, which was heavily influenced by the presence of the U.S. military bases in the Philippines. The story revolves around Michael, a young Filipino man born to a Filipino mother and an American soldier. The novel deals with issues of racial discrimination, identity struggles, and the impact of colonialism on Filipino society, particularly in a community that lives side by side with U.S. military personnel.

Key Themes:
  • Racial Discrimination: Michael faces discrimination both from the American soldiers and Filipinos due to his mixed-race heritage.
  • Identity Struggles: The novel explores Michael's identity crisis, torn between two cultures.
  • Colonial Legacy: It critiques the continuing effects of the U.S. military presence in the Philippines, highlighting economic and cultural dependence.
Characters:
  • Michael: The protagonist, a young man of mixed Filipino and American descent, struggling with his identity.
  • Michael's Mother: A Filipino woman who had a relationship with an American soldier, which resulted in Michael's birth.
  • Other Filipino Characters: The townspeople who experience the effects of U.S. military presence.


Dekada '70 (1983)

Settings:

The story takes place during the 1970s, a turbulent period in Philippine history when Martial Law was declared by President Ferdinand Marcos. The country was under political repression, and activism and protests were widespread.

Main Character:

Amanda Bartolome, a mother of five sons, is the central figure of the story. Through her, we see the effects of the political unrest on the Filipino family.

Themes Explored:
  • Martial Law: The novel addresses the personal and social impact of Martial Law on families, particularly women.
  • Political Activism: As Amanda's children become involved in activism, the novel explores how young people challenged the political status quo.
  • Women's Awakening: Amanda starts as a traditional housewife but gradually becomes more aware of the political struggles around her, reflecting the role of women in the fight for justice.
  • Family Struggles: The ideological divides within the family reflect the national struggles during Martial Law. The Bartolome family is broken apart because of their differing political views.
Plot:

The novel follows the Bartolome family's experiences during Martial Law. Amanda's sons become involved in activism, fighting for freedom and justice against the oppressive government. Amanda herself undergoes a personal transformation, realizing the importance of standing up for her beliefs and supporting her children's causes. Through the family's struggles, Dekada '70 portrays the complexity of living in a politically charged environment and the sacrifices made for the fight for democracy.
